About

Introduction.

I am a fourth year undergraduate student attending the University of California, San Diego. I am graduating Spring 2024 with a B.S. in Environmental Systems. I am an active student researcher at the Scripps Institute of Oceanography in Dr. Katharine Ricke's lab where I study the potential hydrological impact of Marine Cloud Brightening on Coastal Western North America. I am frequently using the Python to utilize a fully-coupled earth systems model to guage potential impact of solar geoengineering in present and future scenarios.

Prior to UC San Diego, I attended community college and transferred from Irvine Valley College (IVC) as a first generation student. As I entered my first years of college at the height of the pandemic, I jumped at the opportunity to do undergraduate research in my second year at IVC. Through a program hosted by Chapman University, I was able to do marine ecology research in the SEACR Lab with Dr. Richelle Tanner. I am currently working on publishing a manuscript for the data collected in Summer 2022.

Environmental Systems Senior Internship

Marine Cloud Brightening and its Regional Impact on Drought Conditions in Coastal Western North America

The first part of the study looks at local variations in drought variables in Coastal Western North America if Marine Cloud Brightening (MCB) were to be deployed to create a cooling response.

◦ 2010 and 2050 conditions were tested to see how the potential deployment of MCB can influence climate-impacted drought varaibles.
◦ Next we will be testing the same drought variables on a global scale and will be using it as comparison.
